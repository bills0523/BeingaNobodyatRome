DATE: A.D. 89

TEXT:
You are born in a poor village in central Italy.
You are the second son, the fourth child.
The poor land of your father cannot support all of you.
NEXT: blacksmith_100

---
DATE: A.D. 100
ID: blacksmith_100
TEXT:
Your parents discussed.
Staying is not an option for you.
Ostia is where you will be sent.
Not quite an opportunity.
You did not know what you would be facing.
You can’t blame your family.
The extra mouth is a burden.
You kept your tears.
You farewell your family.
You step on your own journey at the age of 11.
NEXT: blacksmith_101

---
DATE: A.D. 101
ID: blacksmith_101
TEXT:
It feels like humiliation.
You carry charcoal.
You work the bellows.
You scoop water.
All these dirty works.
You obey, obey, and obey.
NEXT: blacksmith_103

---
DATE: A.D. 103
ID: blacksmith_103
TEXT:
You are blessed.
Your master values your diligence.
Luckily, you are smart enough.
You learn to hold tongs.
You read heat by color.
You have a chance to craft small things.
A lock, a tool, a door hinge.
You enjoyed this.
NEXT: blacksmith_104

---
DATE: A.D. 104
ID: blacksmith_104
TEXT:
You worship Vulcan.
You joined Vulcanalia.
You learned to pray.
He will bless you.
NEXT: blacksmith_105

---
DATE: A.D. 105
ID: blacksmith_105
TEXT:
You are trusted more.
You realized.
Any mistakes, misses, or wrong timing cost your master.
You are talented.
You never miss.
NEXT: blacksmith_106

---
DATE: A.D. 106
ID: blacksmith_106
TEXT:
Trajan’s victory brought opportunities.
They want ironworks.
They want too much, so you see a chance.
Over the years, you learned skills.
How to craft, how to repair, how to adjust.
You earned freedom with your savings.
Your master looked at you with pride.
But also with some worry.
You established your own blacksmith.
For the first time, your labor brings gold to your own pocket.
NEXT: blacksmith_109

---
DATE: A.D. 109
ID: blacksmith_109
TEXT:
You expanded your shop.
You would do anything.
You fit wagons, make tools, craft swords.
You wouldn’t know how much blood your sword will touch.
You wouldn’t care.
You didn’t know how your family was.
You didn’t know whether to hate them or appreciate them.
NEXT: blacksmith_112

---
DATE: A.D. 112
ID: blacksmith_112
TEXT:
You embraced your first apprentice.
You see yourself in his immature face.
You were stunned for a second.
When he was sent by his family.
A different feeling emerges inside you.
A little bit bitter, a little bit sad.
You decide to treat him:

- Like how your master treats you => blacksmith_master_path_114

---
DATE: A.D. 114
ID: blacksmith_master_path_114
TEXT:
He learned quickly, even better than you.
He learned how to read color.
He learned how to finish a blade.
You still sent labor work, the dirty one, to him.
You see him sweating, suffering, but never complaining.
You felt a little uneasy.
You shook away that thought.
You kept working on the immersive orders.
NEXT: blacksmith_master_path_121

---
DATE: A.D. 121
ID: blacksmith_master_path_121
TEXT:
He left, as you did when you were young.
He opened a shop right across from you.
You didn’t worry, because half of his shop belongs to you, too.
You did less and less work.
No more blades, no more wagons, just some casual things only.
NEXT: blacksmith_master_path_129

---
DATE: A.D. 129
ID: blacksmith_master_path_129
TEXT:
You felt the furnace smoke in your lungs.
It burns.
You can no longer lift, you can no longer craft.
You felt death near you.
NEXT: blacksmith_master_path_130

---
DATE: A.D. 130
ID: blacksmith_master_path_130
TEXT:
Your student buries you.
You have no family, no children, no one.
He puts a hammer next to you.
He blesses you with the honor of Vulcan.

ID: blacksmith_son_path_114

You didn’t use him as your master used you.
You taught him skills.
How to read fire.
How to read timing.
How to craft pieces.
You sometimes see a smile on his face.
That’s what was not on you.

He crafts better than you.
Faster, neater, stronger.

DATE: A.D. 120
ID: blacksmith_son_path_120

He works with passion.
He enjoyed crafting.
NEXT: blacksmith_son_path_121

---
DATE: A.D. 121
ID: blacksmith_son_path_121
TEXT:
You stopped crafting.
You work with numbers.
You are backing up.
NEXT: blacksmith_son_path_125

---
DATE: A.D. 125
ID: blacksmith_son_path_125
TEXT:
Illness and furnace smoke didn’t catch you like other experienced masters.
You sometimes still wander in your store, but you no longer work.
He worked well, really well.
You see him taking apprentices and treating them well.
You felt accomplished.
It’s like seeing your son having a family.
You felt pride.

Your name stays with your store forever.
