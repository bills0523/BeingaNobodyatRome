TEXT:
Fortuna does not only decide what you become.
She also decides where you begin.

Will you be born poor, with only your voice and memory to carry you?
Or will you be born rich, protected by family, education, and name?

You grew with love.

DATE: 93 B.C.
ID: poet_poor_93
TEXT:
God blessed you with a good memory and a beautiful voice.
People know you in town, and people recognize you by voice.
You sing to your mother, and she praises.
You sing to your father, but he’s too tired to enjoy.
Your blessed voice took away your talent in body.
You are skinny, and you barely work.
You see your father frowning sometimes.
And you understand what it means.

DATE: 90 B.C.
ID: poet_poor_90
TEXT:
A wealthier family in your town invited you.
They are meeting with a famous merchant from the city.
You joined their dinner.
But you stand aside.
You sing to entertain, which is the reason you’re invited.
You see ignorance in their eyes.
Both the host and the guest.
You kept singing.
Because this is your first earning.
Because you want to make your father proud.
Because you know your voice is why you matter.

DATE: 84 B.C.
ID: poet_poor_84
TEXT:
You started to write some short lines on your own.
You still sing, and you still recite.
People are more generous in the city.
You wish to live by your pen more than by your mouth.
You wander from place to place and hope one day you can settle down.
You are sometimes invited to a rich house.
By there you sell your voice.
You hate to see their face.
They show no respect.

A servant took over you.
He showed you to your father.
His face glows with happiness.
