TITLE: The Fighter
ROLE: gladiator
START: fighter_start

---
DATE: 83 B.C.
ID: fighter_start
TEXT:
You are born into a family in a small tribe.
Your mother luckily did not die during childbirth.
Your father, looking at you as a boy, laughed from the bottom of his heart.
You heard him talking to people around him.
You don’t understand what he was saying, but you can see the pride in his eyes.
NEXT: fighter_80

---
DATE: 80 B.C.
ID: fighter_80
TEXT:
You can barely talk; the old man in the tribe taught you words.
You enjoyed it.
One day, you run into your father’s meeting.
You see his face frowning, and you hear some words.
You didn’t fully understand, but you see the anger in his eyes.
NEXT: fighter_76

---
DATE: 76 B.C.
ID: fighter_76
TEXT:
Your tribe was invaded.
Your father, teacher, cousins, and most people you know have died.
You see them pierced by arrows, or chopped by swords, all covered in blood.
You see your mother lying on the ground, facing down.
You nudged her arm, trying to wake her up.
You see something shiny on your left.
You lift your head up, and you see a Roman soldier.
He pointed his spear at you, and you looked him in the eye.
He put his shield down and picked you up by your neck.
You didn’t cry.
You were brought to the Roman troops.
NEXT: fighter_75

---
DATE: 75 B.C.
ID: fighter_75
TEXT:
Your year in the Roman army wasn’t easy.
You belong to calones.
You need to carry wood for their needs and do all the dirty work.
They yell, they whip, and you see a traitor, your “friend” in calones, died midway through his escape.
NEXT: fighter_74

---
DATE: 74 B.C.
ID: fighter_74
TEXT:
They brought you back to Rome.
It is a huge city, and you see houses twice as big as the one you lived in.
You are auctioned.
You stand on the stage, naked.
The merchants examine you, one by one.
You felt their touches, and you closed your eyes.
You felt stabbing pain in your neck.
You opened your eyes, and you are picked.
The merchant fancied your muscles.
He handed you a piece of bread and told you to be a gladiator.
You listened to him bragging, while wolfing down the piece of bread, you needed it.
You are sent to the gladiatorial school.
NEXT: fighter_73_choice

---
DATE: 73 B.C.
ID: fighter_73_choice
TEXT:
Capua.

You see many men here.
Gauls, Thracians, and many from different places.
Some are fierce, some are violent, some only want blood.
You began training.
A wooden sword and a crappy shield are all you have.
You were undefeated.
Of course you were, or otherwise you would not be alive.
You meet friends, true friends, the ones you trained with.
But you also know, at some time, only one of you will die.
Among your friends, one is named Spartacus.
He whispered:
“Do you hate?”
CHOICES:
- Yes => fighter_rebellion_73
- No => fighter_school_72

---
DATE: 73 B.C.
ID: fighter_rebellion_73
TEXT:
Your plots are discovered.
There's no more time.
You all grabbed whatever you could.
Kitchen knives, spits, sticks.
You killed your way out.
You all climbed to Versius.
You are all surrounded by Roman troops.
They tried to trap you all with hunger, thirst, and fear.
You descend a wild vine rope, sending warriors down to the Roman troops at night.
You all destroyed the camp.
You see one of the captive familiar.
Memory stroke you 10 years ago.
He was the one pushing a dagger into your mom’s heart.
He begged you for mercy.
“I’m not Roman, I’m forced to be in the army.”
You grasped your dagger, and your arm started shaking.
You cut his throat.
NEXT: fighter_rebellion_72

---
DATE: 72 B.C.
ID: fighter_rebellion_72
TEXT:
The leaders do not all agree.
You followed Spartacus for the passion in his eyes.
Crixus, a dear leader in the rebellion, is killed.
You honor him, but you see your future on his dead body.
NEXT: fighter_rebellion_71_choice

---
DATE: 71 B.C.
ID: fighter_rebellion_71_choice
TEXT:
Marcus Licinius Crassus took over the Roman troops.
You felt the war with them tighter, harder, and colder.
The final battle comes.
Before it began, Spartacus killed his horse.
If you all win, he shall gain a better one.
If you all lose, he will need no one.
No one is leaving this field alive.
You clenched your dagger, the one accompanied you since the start.
You rush toward the Romans with your friends.

Your body is covered with blood.
It was an honor.
You are not a failure.

ID: fighter_school_67
TEXT:
The wounds are a burden.

DATE: 66 B.C.
ID: fighter_school_66
TEXT:
You stand in the amphitheater.
A man standing on the opposite side.
He is younger, has no injuries, and is stronger.
You barely blocked his swing.
Your attack is no longer sharpened.
He knocked off your shield.
You see his blade piercing through your chest.
You fell.
The crowd curses.
The crowd yells.
You closed your eyes.
